GDP Per Capita Across South East Asia

South East Asia is a region of diverse economies with countries like Singapore boasting a high GDP per capita due to its developed and diversified economy, while others like Myanmar have a lower GDP per capita reflecting a less developed economic status. For instance, as of 2023, Singapore has a projected GDP per capita of around $63,987, while Myanmar's GDP per capita is about $1,408. This disparity is a reflection of the different stages of economic development and the various challenges and opportunities faced by these countries.

Unemployment Rate Across South East Asia

This bar graph highlights that a high GDP per capita does not always correlate with a low unemployment rate, as exemplified by Brunei. This visualization underscores the varied economic and labor market conditions in the region, encouraging a deeper analysis beyond GDP figures to understand unemployment rates in different South East Asian countries.

GDP Breakdown by Sector

The treemap visualization in the third section provides a detailed breakdown of GDP by sectors (Agriculture, Industry, and Services) across various South East Asian countries, showcasing a diverse range of economic structures. For instance, Singapore significantly leans towards the service sector, contributing to 75.2% of its GDP, while its agricultural sector is non-existent. On the other hand, countries like Cambodia and Laos have a notable portion of their GDP coming from agriculture. Most countries exhibit a balanced contribution from both the Industry and Services sectors, indicative of a transition towards modern economic structures. The visualization also hints at varying economic transitions within these countries, with a higher percentage of GDP from services and industry sectors possibly indicating a move towards a more modernized economy.

How does the Economy affect Health?

The bubble plot in the last section elucidates the relationship between the density of physicians per thousand people and the life expectancy across different South East Asian countries, with the population size represented by the size of the bubbles. A higher density of physicians generally correlates with a higher life expectancy, indicating better healthcare accessibility and standards. For instance, Singapore, with a higher physician density of 2.29 per thousand, showcases a remarkable life expectancy of 83.1 years. However, the varying bubble sizes elucidate the demographic diversity of the region, which is a crucial factor impacting healthcare systems and policy formulation. Moreover, the size of the bubbles portrays the population size, with countries like Indonesia and the Philippines having noticeably larger bubbles, indicating their substantial population. Interestingly, the visualization also hints at the discrepancy in healthcare resources among populous countries. For instance, despite its large population, the physician density in Indonesia stands at 0.43 per thousand, which is significantly lower compared to Singapore. The contrast in physician density and life expectancy between densely populated and less populated countries like Brunei, reflects the varying healthcare challenges and resource allocation strategies across the region.
