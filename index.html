Development Across South East Asia

Rise of Human Development (1990-2021)

The Human Development Index (HDI) offers a composite measurement that encapsulates three pivotal dimensions of human progress: life expectancy, education, and per capita income levels. It is a scale ranging from 0 (low development) to 1 (high development), and serves as a barometer for understanding the overall well-being and quality of life within nations. As we transition through the years, observe the deepening of colors, a testament to the region's strides in improving health, education, and economic conditions.

Progression of Health Care: Life Expectancy

Life expectancy is one of the most direct indicators of a nation's health and well-being. The dot plot below captures the progression of life expectancy in various Southeast Asian countries over a span of three decades. Nearly all countries in the region experienced a significant rise in life expectancy from 1990 to 2021. This trend underscores the region's collective efforts in bolstering health infrastructure, advancing medical research, and embracing preventative health measures.

Progression of Health Care: Children Mortality

From 1990 to 2021, Southeast Asia has made substantial strides in reducing child mortality, as illustrated by this heatmap. The transformation from lighter shades distributed across the heatmap to deeper shades closer to the bottom of the heatmap over the years signals a consistent and significant decrease in the number of children deaths per 1000 live births.

GDP Comparison and Breakdown

This treemap provides a captivating glance into the composition of Southeast Asia's GDP, categorized by key sectors: Agriculture, Industry, and Services. The dominance of the Services sector across all nations underscores its significance in the modern economies of the region. Notably, while Agriculture has historically been a mainstay for many Southeast Asian countries, its relative share in the GDP seems to have been eclipsed by the other two sectors in several nations.

Cost of Living In Recent Years

This bar chart vividly showcases the cost of living across Southeast Asian countries, benchmarked against a world average index value of 100. At a glance, Singapore stands out as the sole nation surpassing the global mean, indicating a higher cost of living relative to its regional counterparts. Toggle between food, housing & utilities, healthcare, and education price indices, to get a comprehensive view of each nation's economic landscape.
